You've probably heard the warning: "never do banking on cafe Wi-Fi." It was great advice in 2012. In 2026, it's mostly outdated — and the *actual* risks of public networks are different from the ones people worry about.

## What HTTPS already fixed

Nearly every site you use today encrypts traffic end-to-end (that's the padlock in your address bar). Someone sitting on the same network can no longer read your passwords or messages the way they could in the coffee-shop-hacking golden age.

## What still matters

- **Fake hotspots** with convincing names ("Starbucks_Free_WiFi_2") that exist to shove phishing pages at you.
- **Captive portals** asking for way more personal data than a network needs.
- **The metadata** — which sites you visit can still be visible, even when the content is encrypted.
